Overview

SignalFlow’s teams wanted straightforward answers to critical questions such as “Which features drive activation?”, “Where do users drop off?”, and “Which campaigns bring the best customers?” Their legacy analytics stack depended on cookies, manual event tracking, and dashboards that only specialists could interpret. As they expanded into Europe and North America, the cost of maintaining consent banners and compliance overhead kept growing.

By adopting Litlyx, a cookie-free, privacy-first analytics platform with a built-in AI data analyst, SignalFlow gained actionable, trustworthy insights across product, marketing, and customer journeys—all while preserving a frictionless user experience and strong data protection guarantees.

Challenge

  • Cookie banners and consent friction were hurting sign-up conversion rates and damaging the first-time user experience.
  • Their existing analytics stack required engineering support and manual dashboards that non-technical stakeholders rarely used.
  • Answering ad-hoc questions like “Why did trial conversions drop last week?” required exports, spreadsheets, or custom SQL.
  • With growth across multiple regions, they needed to remain fully compliant with GDPR and CCPA without sacrificing visibility into performance.

Solution: Litlyx Cookie-Free, AI-Driven Analytics

SignalFlow integrated Litlyx by adding a lightweight script during deployment. Setup took less than 30 seconds after registration, requiring no cookie banners or extra consent prompts. From there, the team could define custom events and conversion funnels—for example, “Account created → First project created → First team invite → Upgrade to paid”—in just a few clicks.

Using the built-in AI data analyst, product and marketing teams asked natural-language questions such as “Which traffic sources bring the highest activation rate?” or “Where do users most often drop off in onboarding?” Litlyx automatically surfaced anomalies, trends, and optimization opportunities in a single AI-driven dashboard.

All analytics ran on a privacy-first architecture: no cookies, no cross-site tracking, and strict data minimization, with configurations aligned to both GDPR and CCPA requirements.

Implementation Timeline

  1. Day 1 – Setup. Added the Litlyx snippet to the marketing site and in-app pages, enabling automatic tracking for key user actions.
  2. Week 1 – Funnel & Event Design. Defined events for sign-up, onboarding, feature usage, and upgrades, and built funnels for free-to-paid conversion.
  3. Week 2 – Insight Loop. Teams began asking questions directly in Litlyx instead of requesting manual reports. The AI analyst flagged a major onboarding drop-off and identified low-performing channels.
  4. Ongoing – Optimization. Product, growth, and customer success teams iterated on onboarding, landing pages, and experiments, all using the same privacy-safe data foundation.
